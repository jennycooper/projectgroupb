Discover Ireland At Any Time Of Year!

Ireland has much to offer to tourists at all times of the year, regardless of the season.
Spring has the start of the longer days with less crowds, Summer is the ideal time to catch the breathtaking scenery, Autumn has plenty of warm sunny days, and Winter has plenty of cozy pubs, crowd-free historic sights, and great accommodation deals.

Highlights of Ireland Video:

With evening stretching to as late as 10.30pm, summer days give tourists plenty of time to absorb the amazing scenery Ireland has to offer.
Touring around the west coast is best insummer months, as the atlantic coast gets the worst of the rain and winds during the other seasons. Stop along the way to see the Cliffs of Moher, The Burren, Benbulben and the Aran Islands.

Ireland enjoys mild temperatures in the Winter, and although it can rain, there are plenty of indoor sights still to visit regardless of weather.
Catch a medieval experience at Bunratty Castle, enjoy a fresh pint of guinness on a tour of the Guinness Brewery, visit the Titanic Exhibition at the ship's last port of call in Cobh, and don't miss out on the winter solstice at the megalithic tomb at Newgrange.

Take advantage of this slightly quiter period to enjoy some of the highlights Ireland has to offer, without having to queue during the busy summer crowds.
Kiss the Blarney Stone, enjoy a peaceful cruise on the Shannon while it is not congested with boats, take a historic trip to the National Heritage Park to see crannogs, and enjoy the beauty of Christ Church Cathedral and its viking display.

Take a break from the summer crowds and enjoy some of the busier sights without the crowds. Long stretches in the evenings make autumn the perfect outdoor season.
Take a walk in the spectacular scenery of Powersourt Gardens, visit the historic sights at Clonmacnoise, take advantage of the quieter walks through stunning Glendalough, or stroll through Trinity College.
